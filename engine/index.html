Quantitative Engine Documentation

Count Check relies on trailing analytical mathematical models to score volatility, channel deviations, and execution probabilities.

1. The Flipping Analytics Model

Trailing Moving Volatility

We evaluate historical price anomalies using a Linear Time-Decay Weighted Moving Volatility model. This emphasizes recent price fluctuations over historic out-of-boundary noise:

P_w = [ Sum_{i=1}^{N} (P_i * w_i) ] / [ Sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i ]
sigma^2_w = [ Sum_{i=1}^{N} (P_i - P_w)^2 * w_i ] / [ Sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i ]
Where w_i = i/N represents linear weights, and P_i represents sales price vectors.

Relative Channel Position Index (RCPI)

To determine where an item is trading relative to its boundaries, we locate its current price on a normalized 14-day support (P_floor) and resistance (P_ceiling) scale:

RCPI = (P_current - P_floor) / (P_ceiling - P_floor)

If the asset rises above the median threshold (RCPI > 0.5), we define its structural correction probability factor:

Reversion Risk = max(0, (RCPI - 0.5) * 2.0)

Execution Probability Model

The probability of order execution (P_exec) is modeled as a function of liquidity scores, correction risks, volume imbalances, and empirical tracking ratios:

Base Prob = (Liquidity Score^0.75) * (1.0 - Reversion Risk * 0.6) * Horizon Aggressiveness
P_exec = Base Prob * (1.0 + (Imbalance Ratio - 0.5) * 0.4) * lambda
Where Imbalance Ratio = Buy Volume / (Buy Volume + Sell Volume), and lambda represents the empirical scalar from the last 30 mock actions.

Volume Sizing Constraints

To prevent capital lockups on wide-margin, low-liquidity assets, the target order quantity (Q) is strictly bounded:

Q = min( L_buy, B / P_buy, V_blended * alpha * Market Share )
Where L_buy is the buy limit, B is allocated budget, and V_blended = (V_live * 0.4) + (V_historical * 0.6).

The dynamic market share scalar is calculated based on trading velocity thresholds:

Market Share = 
  0.25  if V_blended >= 10,000 units/hr
  0.15  if 1,000 <= V_blended < 10,000
  0.08  if 100 <= V_blended < 1,000
  0.04  if V_blended < 100

2. Autonomous Trading Simulator

Every 300 seconds, the background cycle orchestrator (CycleRunner) executes paper-trading simulations:

Step 1

Limit-Order Fill

Simulated buy orders transition from buying to holding if the active Grand Exchange buy price meets or drops below your entry threshold.

Step 2

Early Profit-Taking

If the active market sell price hits your target exit threshold, the bot liquidates the position instantly, recording a success and adding net profit to your simulated bank.

Step 3

Volatility-Adjusted Stop Loss (VASL)

If market support collapses below a volatility-based threshold, the position is immediately dumped at a loss:

Stop Loss % = max(0.02, min(0.10, (sigma_historical / P_buy) * 1.5))
Step 4

Tax Realization

Standard 2.0% Grand Exchange tax (implemented May 29, 2025, capped at 5,000,000 GP per item) is realized on all sales. Break-even sales are correctly recorded as net-losses equal to the tax.
