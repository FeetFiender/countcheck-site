Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Welcome to the Count Check knowledge base. Below are the most common questions regarding our quantitative portfolio engine, combat simulators, caching infrastructure, and connection parameters.

General & Security

Does the bot connect to my OSRS account or trade with real GP?

No. Count Check is a completely offline, read-only analytical tool. It never asks for, requires, or stores your Jagex account credentials, passwords, or bank PINs. All flipping portfolios, paper-trading ledger transactions, and combat drop simulations are executed within isolated, risk-free mock environments using simulated capital.

Is using Count Check compliant with Jagex's Terms of Service?

Yes. Because the bot operates completely independently of your game client—and does not execute inputs or macro actions inside the game itself—using Count Check is 100% compliant with Jagex's Third-Party Platform guidelines.

Flipping & Portfolio Portfolios

Where does the real-time pricing and volume data come from?

All market metrics, trade limits, and price points are fetched from the official OSRS Wiki Real-Time Prices API. This data is populated by live crowdsourced telemetry from active clients. Our background cycles refresh these figures every 300 seconds to ensure sizers operate on the most accurate spreads possible.

What is the "Conviction" rating on my /flip recommendations?

Conviction is an automated risk-mitigation grade (from A to D) assigned by our quantitative engine:

  • A-Grade: High-liquidity, low-volatility assets with a very high execution probability.
  • B-Grade: Standard margins with solid historical velocity.
  • C-Grade: High-margin, lower-velocity assets.
  • D-Grade: High-volatility or highly illiquid assets that carry an increased risk of price-erosion or unfilled order lockups.

How is the 2.0% Grand Exchange tax calculated?

We strictly enforce the official OSRS tax algorithm. All margins, ROI projections, and packaged sets evaluate the rounded-down 2.0% fee:

Tax = floor( P_sell * 0.02 ) (if P_sell >= 50, capped at 5,000,000 GP)

This ensures that narrow-spread transactions do not recommend losing trades after transaction fees are realized.

Combat & Casket Simulators

Are the drop rates in /kill and /open accurate to the real game?

Yes. All drops, weights, and pre-rolls are configured inside local databases mapped directly from official Jagex source publications and comprehensive Wiki crowd-data. This includes symmetrical tertiary rolls (such as clues and pets), exact minigame reward caskets, and Varlamore reward tables.

Why do some item icons or drops stitch side-by-side?

When the simulator registers unique milestone achievements (such as obtaining an Olmlet pet alongside a high-value Chambers of Xeric purple chest item), the engine dynamically stitches the two independent transparent sprites side-by-side onto a customized OSRS interface slot, upscaling it Nearest-Neighbor to prevent blurred graphics on smartphone viewports.

Player Stats & Cooldowns

Why is there a 3-hour cooldown on /temple update?

To protect our server's hosting IP address from being blacklisted or challenged by Cloudflare security layers, we enforce a strict 3-hour (10,800 seconds) local cooldown per player name. This prevents rate-limit spam while ensuring that Temple OSRS and Jagex have enough window space to scrape and log fresh datapoints safely.

How does the bot calculate Combat Levels inside /jagex stats?

The bot calculates your exact OSRS combat level using Jagex's official mathematical steps:

Base = 0.25 * (Defence + Hitpoints + floor(Prayer * 0.5))
Melee = 0.325 * (Attack + Strength)
RangedEffective = 0.325 * floor(Ranged * 1.5)
MagicEffective = 0.325 * floor(Magic * 1.5)
Combat Level = floor(Base + max(Melee, RangedEffective, MagicEffective))

Why did my command return a "This command is on cooldown" error?

To guarantee server stability and prevent malicious actors from flooding the host with massive rendering tasks, we enforce per-user rate limiting:

  • Heavy Simulator Commands (/flip, /f2p, /analyze, /compare, /kill, /clue, /open, /graph): Restricted to 1 execution per 5 seconds per user.
  • General Commands: Restricted to 1 execution per 1 second per user to block double-click spam.
  • /debug Submissions: Restricted to 1 execution per 30 seconds per user to protect local database logs.
