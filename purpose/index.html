Purpose & Goals

Count Check was engineered to solve the real-time economic, mechanical, and statistical challenges faced by Old School RuneScape players and merchants. From casual adventurers seeking to optimize their boss grinds to quantitative merchants executing high-volume capital flips, Count Check bridges the gap between raw game telemetry and smart decision-making.

Operational Note

This project will be open source once beta testing is complete. For the duration of the beta, you'll need to use our hosted bot. You can interact with it in our server, invite it to your own server, or use it directly through DMs.

1. Information Fragmentation & RNG Uncertainty (Basic Level)

Scattered Player Profiles and Logs

The Player's Problem:Tracking stats, boss counts, and collection logs requires opening multiple separate platforms (Wiki pages, RuneLite client panes, Jagex Hiscores, and Temple OSRS). There is no single, unified dashboard to view a player's complete profile and progression.

The Count Check Solution:Consolidates official Jagex index-lite data, Temple OSRS progression metrics, categorized collection logs, and pet tracking into a single, high-definition visual card directly inside Discord.

The RNG "Dry Streak" Illusion

The Player's Problem:Drop rates are abstract numbers. A player preparing to grind a boss (e.g., General Graardor for a Bandos hilt) has no intuitive grasp of RNG variance, potential dry streaks, or cumulative resource yields.

The Count Check Solution:Offers high-fidelity combat and casket simulators (/kill, /clue, /open, /pet) that roll true drops in memory. This gives players an immediate, visual, and statistical representation of drop distributions and dry streaks over thousands of simulated completions.

2. Grand Exchange Friction & Arbitrage Tedium (Intermediate Level)

The 2.0% Grand Exchange Tax Barrier

The Player's Problem:With the implementation of the 2.0% Grand Exchange tax, calculating trading profit is no longer a matter of simple subtraction. Guesswork on high-value items often leads to "tax slippage," where narrow margins are completely eaten by transaction fees, turning paper gains into net-GP losses.

The Count Check Solution:Automatically executes Rounded-Down Tax Math across all portfolios and analytical calculations, while accounting for tax-exempt items (such as runes and ammo) to guarantee true profitability:

Tax = floor( P_sell * 0.02 ) (if P_sell >= 50, capped at 5,000,000 GP)

Tedious Arbitrage Calculations

The Player's Problem:High alchemy, potion decanting (converting 3-dose to 4-dose via Bob Barter), and set packaging are highly lucrative, risk-free activities. However, manually checking raw piece prices, accounting for buy limits, and calculating tax-adjusted margins for dozens of items is too tedious for most players to do daily.

The Count Check Solution:Provides instant, automated scanners (/potion, /sets, /alch) that evaluate every tradeable potion, gear set, and alchemy target on the market, presenting the highest-yield loops sorted by direct ROI and limits.

Low-Velocity Margin Traps

The Player's Problem:Many items look profitable on paper due to wide bid-ask spreads, but they have extremely low transaction velocity. Merch players often find their capital locked up in buy offers that never fill, or end up holding items they cannot liquidate.

The Count Check Solution:Uses blended-velocity analytics (combining live 1-hour volume with 7-day trailing historical averages) to automatically filter out low-volume market-manipulation traps.

3. Portfolio Sizing & Volatility Exposure (Complex Level)

Overexposure & Allocation Failures

The Player's Problem:Most OSRS players do not know how to size their trade offers relative to their cash stack. They often make the mistake of going "all-in" on a single item, exposing their entire bank to sudden market crashes or long holding periods.

The Count Check Solution:Features a Quantitative Portfolio Sizer (/flip, /f2p) that distributes capital across multiple asset classes (such as runes, consumable commodities, and PvM gear). It enforces dynamic sizer caps and concentration limits to ensure portfolios are perfectly calibrated to the player's active budget.

Catching a "Falling Knife" (Reversion Risk)

The Player's Problem:Players often buy items at the absolute peak of their 14-day price cycle, right before a natural market correction occurs.

The Count Check Solution:Uses the Relative Channel Position Index (RCPI) to locate an item’s current price relative to its 14-day floor and ceiling. If an item is overvalued, the engine calculates a Reversion Risk modifier to warn the player against buying into a correction.

Price Erosion & Undercutting (Slippage over Time)

The Player's Problem:Offers left on the Grand Exchange are subject to competition. Opposing traders constantly undercut prices, causing the target margin to erode the longer an offer sits un-filled.

The Count Check Solution:Simulates an Erosion Forecast Timeline (/analyze Page 3) to model exactly how much value is expected to slip over time due to competitor activity, outputting the optimal entry and exit windows for order placement.
